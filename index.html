about me

Hi! My name is Neil Malur, and I'm a rising junior at Weston High School in Weston, MA. I'm interested in computer science, computational biology, and the applications of machine learning, and I've been working on projects involving those fields in my free time. I'm also interested in business and mathematics, and I regularly conduct original research, many of which have been published or received awards. In my free time I also work as a software developer intern at KTByte, a computer science education company, and I volunteer to teach middle school students about robotics and debate.

I conduct research at the Harvard-MIT Biomedical Cybernetics Laboratory under the supervision of Prof. Gil Alterovitz, developing a machine learning methodology to improve matching for patients to clinical trials, which have notoriously low participation rates. I've also interned at places like Columbia Threadneedle Investments and KTByte, working on both frontend and backend work with big data. At these positions, I've worked with standard workplace languages and environments such as Scala, Salesforce, SQL, twig, HTML, Javascript, CSS, and more.
